Editorial Guidelines

Document Structure and Metadata

Every XML document will bear a name directly correlating to its content or identification number, ensuring straightforward retrieval and recognition (e.g., "monroe_poem_002.xml").

The <teiHeader> will encapsulate exhaustive metadata, detailing the document's title, authorship, encoding responsibilities, publication specifics, and the original document's provenance, to furnish clear and actionable information.

It will articulate the digital edition's name, URL, publisher details, and the licensing terms under which the edition is disseminated.

Transcription Principles

All characters will be transcribed as accurately as possible, honoring the original spelling, punctuation, and lineation. Modifications or annotations made by Monroe or subsequent editors will be precisely indicated.

Deletions and additions will be distinctly marked, maintaining the document's authentic attributes.

The structure of the text will be encoded with fidelity, utilizing TEI elements to reflect the text's organization and format.

Editorial decisions, contextual information, or interpretations will be communicated through <note> elements, elucidating on the editorial rationale or elucidating ambiguous or compromised text sections.

Physical Description

The original manuscript's physical characteristics will be exhaustively described, addressing the support type, dimensions, handwriting style, and any pertinent physical features critical for understanding the text's material existence.

Whenever images of manuscript pages are accessible, they will be linked, enabling direct comparison between the transcription and the original manuscript.

Editorial Revisions and Change Log

The <revisionDesc> element will document all significant editorial changes, ensuring editorial process transparency and facilitating historical documentation of the document's editorial evolution.

Accessibility and Usability

These guidelines will be readily accessible to all project participants and potential collaborators, with periodic reviews and updates to incorporate new insights or evolving best practices.

A uniform application of these guidelines across all project documents will be maintained, promoting a cohesive and user-friendly digital edition.

Legal and Ethical Considerations

The copyright status of the original documents and the digital edition, including usage or reproduction restrictions, will be clearly stated, adhering to specified licensing conditions (e.g., Creative Commons).
